Rome & Venice

Italy has always been one of my dream destinations because of its rich history, breathtaking architecture, and timeless charm. I’ve always been fascinated by ancient civilizations, and Rome, the heart of the Roman Empire, feels like the perfect place to experience history firsthand. Venice, on the other hand, feels like a city straight out of a fairy tale with its picturesque canals and romantic atmosphere. Whether it’s standing in the middle of the Colosseum, taking a classic Leaning Tower of Pisa photo, or gliding through the Grand Canal in a gondola, Italy is a place I’ve always wanted to explore.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is one of those landmarks that I’ve always seen in history books, movies, and documentaries, but I know that nothing compares to seeing it in person. Just imagining how thousands of people once gathered there to watch gladiators fight is incredible. I love visiting places with deep historical significance, and standing inside an arena that has existed for nearly two thousand years would be unforgettable.

How to Get to Italy:

  • Connecting Flights: Traveling to Italy from the Philippines requires a connecting flight since there are no direct routes. The best option is a one-stop flight via major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul landing at Leonardo da Vinci International Airport in Rome.
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Turkish Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 18-22 hours, including layovers.

Getting to the Colosseum

  • To the Colosseum: Take Metro Line B to Colosseo Station, which is just a short walk from the site.

Leaning Tower of Pisa

I’ve always wanted to visit the Leaning Tower of Pisa, not just for the classic “holding up the tower” photo, which I would definitely take, but also because it’s such a fascinating piece of history. The fact that this tower has been standing for centuries despite its tilt amazes me. Plus, Pisa itself looks like a charming city worth exploring. Walking through its streets, trying some authentic Italian gelato, and taking in the unique atmosphere would make the trip even more special.

How to Get to Italy:

  • Connecting Flights: Traveling to Italy from the Philippines requires a connecting flight since there are no direct routes. The best option is a one-stop flight via major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul landing at Leonardo da Vinci International Airport in Rome.
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Turkish Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 18-22 hours, including layovers.

Getting to the Leaning Tower of Pisa

  • To Pisa: Take a train from Rome to Pisa Centrale, then either walk or take a bus to the Leaning Tower.

Grand Canal

Venice has always seemed like one of the most magical places in the world to me. There’s something so romantic and enchanting about a city built on water, where boats replace cars and every turn leads to a scenic view. Taking a gondola ride through the Grand Canal is one of those bucket-list experiences I’ve dreamed about for years. Exploring the narrow streets, visiting small cafés, and simply getting lost in the beauty of Venice would be an experience unlike any other.

How to Get to Italy:

  • Connecting Flights: Traveling to Italy from the Philippines requires a connecting flight since there are no direct routes. The best option is a one-stop flight via major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul landing at Leonardo da Vinci International Airport in Rome.
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Turkish Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 18-22 hours, including layovers.

Getting to the Grand Canal:

  • To Venice: Ride a high-speed train from Rome to Venice, which takes around 3.5 to 4 hours.

Other Places to Visit

Milan

The city’s most iconic landmark is the Duomo di Milano, the largest cathedral in Italy and one of the most intricate Gothic structures in the world, taking nearly six centuries to complete.

Pompeii

Located near Naples, Pompeii is one of the most important archaeological sites in the world. It was a thriving Roman city before it was buried under volcanic ash and pumice after the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD.

Florence

Known as the birthplace of the Renaissance, Florence is one of the most culturally significant cities in Italy. It is home to world-famous museums and landmarks, including the Uffizi Gallery, which houses masterpieces by Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael.
