BIG BEN BUCKINGHAM PALACE STONEHENGE

England

The UK is full of iconic landmarks, royal history, and beautiful countryside. London, the capital of the UK, is a city that has been featured in countless books and films, making it one of the most recognizable cities in the world. From London’s historic sites to the mystery of Stonehenge, I’ve always wanted to explore this fascinating country. London is home to many well-known places that I’ve only seen in books and movies. I want to visit historical landmarks like Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, and the Stonehenge to learn more about British history. I’m also interested in exploring the city’s museums, markets, and shopping areas.

The Big Ben

One of the most iconic landmarks is Big Ben, a clock tower that has become a symbol of London. I’ve seen countless images and videos of Big Ben, but standing before it and hearing its chimes would make me feel truly connected to London. Seeing this iconic clock tower in person would be a dream come true.

How to Get to the UK:

  • Connecting Flights: There are no direct flights from Manila to England, but one can take a one-stop flight via Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul.
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and British Airways offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 16-20 hours, including layovers.

Getting Around London

  • To Big Ben: The easiest way to get around London is by using the London Underground. The Westminster station is the closest stop for Big Ben.

The Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the British monarch. The palace is one of the most visited attractions in London, especially during the Changing of the Guard ceremony. Witnessing this tradition and learning more about the royal family would be fascinating.

The Stonehenge

Stonehenge is one of the most mysterious ancient monuments in the world, and I have always been curious about it. Located in Wiltshire, England, this prehistoric stone circle has puzzled historians and archaeologists for centuries. The mystery behind these massive stones is what makes the site so fascinating. I would love to walk around the area and learn more about the different interpretations of how the stones were transported and arranged.

Getting Around Stonehenge:

  • To Stonehenge: The best way to reach Stonehenge is to take a train from London to Salisbury, followed by a bus ride to the Stonehenge Visitor Centre.

Other Places to Visit

Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh, Scotland, a city famous for its medieval charm, historic castles, and stunning landscapes. The Edinburgh Castle, located on a volcanic rock, is one of the most visited attractions in Scotland.

Oxford

Oxford, home to one of the world’s most prestigious universities. Oxford University has a beautiful campus with historic buildings and libraries that date back centuries. Walking through its cobblestone streets and visiting its famous colleges would be an amazing experience.

The Lake District

The Lake District, a national park in northwest England known for its breathtaking scenery. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers, with its scenic lakes, rolling hills, and hiking trails.
