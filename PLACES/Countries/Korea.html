South Korea

Jenilyn Chua

MYEONG-DONG LOTTE WORLD SMTOWN

Seoul

Seoul is a city that has captivated me for years, and visiting it would be a dream come true. Its seamless blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge technology makes it a place like no other. What draws me to Seoul the most is my love for K-pop, Korean dramas, and Korean food, all of which have made me feel connected to the culture even from afar. Walking through the vibrant streets, experiencing the fast-paced city life, and immersing myself in the things I love about Korea would make this trip incredibly special.

Myeong-Dong

Myeong-dong is one of the most exciting districts in Seoul, known for its endless shopping streets, dazzling lights, and mouthwatering street food. One of my must-dos here is to try authentic Korean street food. I’d love to taste tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), hotteok (sweet pancakes), odeng (fish cakes), and Korean fried chicken straight from the source. The idea of hopping from one stall to another, trying different flavors, and soaking in the lively atmosphere excites me.

How to Get to South Korea:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach South Korea from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Incheon International Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Korean Air offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4 hours.

Getting to Myeong-Dong

  • To Myeong-dong: Take the Airport Railroad Express to Seoul Station, then transfer to Line 4 to Myeong-dong Station.

Lotte World

I’ve always wanted to experience Lotte World, one of the most famous amusement parks in Korea. Unlike other theme parks, Lotte World has both an indoor and outdoor section, making it unique. I’d start by exploring Magic Island, the outdoor area, where I could take breathtaking photos in front of the Lotte World Castle. The Atlantis roller coaster is at the top of my must-ride list since it’s known for its thrilling speed and twists. Inside the park, I’d enjoy the fantasy-like atmosphere, watch the parades, and try fun attractions. Lotte World is a place that would bring out my inner child and create unforgettable memories.

How to Get to South Korea:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach South Korea from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Incheon International Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Korean Air offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4 hours.

Getting to the Lotte World

  • To Lotte World: Take Line 2 to Jamsil Station, which leads directly to the park

SM Town

As a dedicated K-pop fan, visiting SM Town would be like stepping into a dream. SM Entertainment is home to some of the most legendary K-pop artists, and being able to explore a place dedicated to them would be unforgettable. The gallery inside SM Town showcases costumes, albums, and memorabilia from various idols, giving fans like me a deeper appreciation of the industry. I’d also love to visit the gift shop and get my hands on exclusive merchandise. If I’m lucky, I might even catch a live event, a fan meeting, or even spot an idol! Just standing in a place that has played such a big role in K-pop history would be a surreal moment for me.

How to Get to South Korea:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach South Korea from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Incheon International Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Korean Air offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4 hours.

Getting to the SM Town:

  • To SM Town: Take the subway to COEX Artium in Gangnam, where SM Town is located.

Other Places to Visit

N Seoul Tower

One of Seoul’s most famous landmarks, offering panoramic views of the city. Visitors can take a cable car up to the tower and enjoy attractions like the love locks area and digital observatory.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

The largest and most iconic palace in Seoul, known for its beautiful architecture and historical significance. The Royal Guard Changing Ceremony is a popular attraction for tourists.

Nami Island

A beautiful island famous for its tree-lined paths, stunning landscapes, and peaceful atmosphere. It is especially popular during autumn and winter and is well-known as a filming location for K-dramas. Visitors can explore the island by bike, take a ferry ride, or enjoy nature walks.
