Paris

Paris has always felt like a dream destination to me—a city filled with romance, art, and timeless elegance. Every aspect of Paris, from its grand boulevards to its charming cafés, seems to tell a story of history and culture. The idea of experiencing the vibrant energy of Paris firsthand, with its rich artistic heritage and iconic landmarks, is something I’ve longed for. Whether it's the excitement of Disneyland, the awe-inspiring view from the Eiffel Tower, or the haunting beauty of the Catacombs, Paris has so much to offer, and I can’t wait to explore it all.

Disneyland Paris

A trip to Paris wouldn’t feel complete without visiting Disneyland Paris, a place where childhood dreams come to life. Ever since I was young, I’ve been captivated by Disney’s magical worlds. The park’s thrilling rides, enchanting parades, and nighttime fireworks show would make the visit truly unforgettable.

How to Get to France:

  • Connecting Flights: Traveling to Paris from the Philippines requires a connecting flight since there are no direct routes. The best option is a one-stop flight via major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul before arriving at Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Singapore Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 16-20 hours, including layovers.

Getting to Disneyland Paris

  • To Disneyland Paris: The fastest way is to take the TGV train from Charles de Gaulle Airport directly to Marne-la-Vallée station, which is right at the entrance of Disneyland.

The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most famous landmarks in the world, and seeing it in person would be a surreal experience. I’ve always imagined standing beneath its towering structure, taking in the breathtaking sight that has been the backdrop of so many movies and postcards. The Eiffel Tower represents the heart of Paris, and being there would make me feel like I’ve truly arrived in the city. Seeing it sparkle at night is something I’ve always dreamed of, and I can’t wait to experience its magic.

How to Get to France:

  • Connecting Flights: Traveling to Paris from the Philippines requires a connecting flight since there are no direct routes. The best option is a one-stop flight via major international hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul before arriving at Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris
  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Singapore Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 16-20 hours, including layovers.

Getting to the Eiffel Tower

  • To the Eiffel Tower: Take the RER C train to Champ de Mars - Tour Eiffel station, which is just a short walk from the tower.

Paris Catacombs

The Paris Catacombs offer a darker, more mysterious side of the city, which has always intrigued me. Walking through tunnels lined with carefully arranged bones from centuries past would be both chilling and fascinating. The Catacombs hold so much history, and I am eager to learn about why they were created and how they became one of Paris’s most unique attractions. I love exploring places that have an air of mystery, and visiting the Catacombs would give me a deeper understanding of Paris beyond its romantic and glamorous image.

How to Get to France:

  • Airlines: Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Singapore Airlines offer connecting flights, with a total travel time of 16-20 hours, including layovers.

Getting to the Paris Catacombs:

  • To the Catacombs: Take Metro Line 4 to Denfert-Rochereau Station, which is located near the entrance to the Catacombs.

Other Places to Visit

The Louvre

One of the most famous museums in the world, the Louvre houses thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.

Champs-Élysées & Arc de Triomphe

The Avenue des Champs-Élysées is known for its luxury shops, cafés, and theaters, making it one of the most vibrant streets in Paris. At its western end stands the Arc de Triomphe, a monument honoring those who fought and died for France.

Palace of Versailles

A symbol of France’s royal history, the Palace of Versailles is renowned for its opulent architecture, extravagant interiors, and beautifully landscaped gardens. The Hall of Mirrors, where the Treaty of Versailles was signed, is one of its most famous features.
