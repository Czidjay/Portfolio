DISNEYLAND TOKYO UNIVERSAL STUDIOS MT. FUJI

Tokyo & Osaka

Japan has always fascinated me because of its ability to balance tradition and modernity so seamlessly. Tokyo, in particular, feels like a city where every corner offers something unique. Meanwhile, Osaka is known for its lively atmosphere, delicious street food, and fun attractions. I’ve long been drawn to Japan through its entertainment, food, and innovative technology, so visiting both Tokyo and Osaka would be a dream come true. I can already imagine myself exploring the city, trying authentic Japanese cuisine, and immersing myself in everything from pop culture to breathtaking natural scenery.

Disneyland Tokyo

Growing up watching Disney movies, I’ve always been enchanted by the idea of stepping into a real-life fairytale. Tokyo Disneyland is one of the most magical theme parks in the world, and visiting it would be like reliving my childhood dreams. I’d love to ride Space Mountain, Pirates of the Caribbean, and Big Thunder Mountain, experiencing the thrill of the attractions I’ve only seen online. Exploring Cinderella’s Castle and seeing my favorite Disney characters in parades would make the experience even more magical. But one thing I’m especially excited about is the Disney-themed snacks. From Mickey shaped waffles to Little Green Alien mochi from Toy Story, I’d love to try all the adorable food that Tokyo Disneyland is famous for. The thought of spending an entire day surrounded by Disney’s magic makes this one of my must-visit places in Japan.

How to Get to Japan:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach Japan from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Narita International Airport or Haneda Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Japan Airlines offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4-5 hours.

Getting to Disneyland Tokyo

  • To Disneyland: Take the Keiyo Line from Tokyo Station to Maihama Station, where Disneyland is located.

Universal Studios Japan

Another major destination on my list is Universal Studios Japan, located in Osaka. This theme park is home to the Wizarding World of Harry Potter, which is a dream come true for a fan like me. Walking through Hogsmeade Village, seeing Hogwarts Castle, and sipping on Butterbeer would feel like stepping into the world of the books and movies I’ve loved for so long. I’d definitely want to ride the Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey attraction. But aside from Harry Potter, I’m also excited about Super Nintendo World, where I can explore Mario’s world and maybe even race on a real life Mario Kart ride.

How to Get to Japan:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach Japan from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Narita International Airport or Haneda Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Japan Airlines offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4-5 hours.

Getting to Universal Studios Japan

  • To Universal Studios Japan: Take the Shinkansen, bullet train, to Osaka, then transfer to the JR Yumesaki Line to reach the park.

Mount Fuji

One of Japan’s most famous landmarks, Mount Fuji, is a place I’ve always wanted to see in person. Whether I hike part of the mountain or admire it from a scenic viewpoint, I know I’d be in awe of its beauty. Seeing its snow-capped peak, reflecting in the surrounding lakes, would be an unforgettable sight. If possible, I’d love to visit during cherry blossom season when the pink flowers frame Mount Fuji, making it look even more breathtaking. Another dream would be to soak in an onsen, aka hot spring, while enjoying a view of the mountain. It would be the perfect way to relax after exploring Tokyo.

How to Get to Japan:

  • Connecting Flights: The best way to reach Japan from Manila would be by taking a direct flight to Narita International Airport or Haneda Airport.
  • Airlines: Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, or Japan Airlines offer direct flights, with a total travel time of 4-5 hours.

Getting to Mount Fuji:

  • To Mount Fuji: Take a highway bus or the Fuji Excursion train from Shinjuku Station to get to the scenic area near the mountain.

Other Places to Visit

Shibuya Crossing

Known as the busiest pedestrian crossing in the world, Shibuya Crossing is an iconic Tokyo landmark. Hundreds of people cross at the same time from multiple directions, creating a fascinating sight that represents the energy of the city.

Akihabara

Akihabara is a major hub for anime, manga, and gaming culture. It is home to numerous electronics shops, themed cafés, and multi-story arcades. Visitors can explore specialty stores for collectibles, experience retro and modern gaming, and enjoy unique themed dining experiences.

Kyoto

The former capital of Japan, Kyoto is famous for its well-preserved traditional architecture, historic temples, and beautiful natural scenery. Kyoto also offers a glimpse into Japan’s cultural heritage with geisha districts and tea ceremonies.
